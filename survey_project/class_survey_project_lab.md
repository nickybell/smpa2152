1.  Use a graph to show **one** topline result (in percentages) from
    this survey. Be sure to include the margins of error on the graph.
    Interpret your results. What have you learned from this analysis?

2.  Use a graph to show **one** cross-tab (in percentages) from this
    survey. Be sure to include the margins of error on the graph.
    Interpret your results. What have you learned from this analysis?

3.  Use a graph to explore **one** demographic variable (e.g., age,
    gender, etc. but **not** class year) in your survey. Use the public
    [GWU Enrollment
    Dashboard](https://irp.gwu.edu/enrollment-dashboard-0) to find the
    composition of the entire student body for that variable. Compare
    that to the distribution in your survey. Which groups were easier or
    harder to reach in this survey? Why do you think that might be?

4.  Please reflect on your experience collecting survey responses from
    your fellow students. What did you learn about the process of
    collecting survey data? What challenges did you encounter? How would
    you approach this differently if you were to do it again? You answer
    should consist of at least one paragraph.
